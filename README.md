# 📊 HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

As part of my Data Analyst Internship at **SkillCraft Technology**, this project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using an interactive Power BI dashboard.

The objective of this task is to explore workforce data and answer a critical business question:

> **Why are employees leaving the organization?**

This dashboard enables users to filter and explore attrition patterns across multiple dimensions such as department, job role, age group, and gender.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used

* **Power BI** – Dashboard creation & visualization
* **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Measures and calculated columns
* **Microsoft Excel / CSV** – Data source

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset used is the **IBM HR Employee Attrition Dataset**, which includes employee-level information such as:

* Age
* Department
* Job Role
* Monthly Income
* Job Satisfaction
* OverTime
* Attrition

This dataset is widely used for HR analytics and attrition prediction.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Build an **interactive and user-friendly dashboard**
* Enable filtering based on:

  * Department
  * Job Role
  * Age Group
  * Gender
* Identify patterns and trends in employee attrition
* Provide insights to answer:

  * **Who is leaving?**
  * **Where is attrition highest?**
  * **Why are employees leaving?**

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### 🔹 KPI Cards

* **Total Employees** – Overall workforce size
* **Attrition Count** – Number of employees who left
* **Attrition Rate (%)** – Percentage of attrition
* **Active Employees** – Employees currently working

---

### 🔹 Interactive Filters (Slicers)

* Department (Dropdown)
* Job Role (Dropdown)
* Age Group (Dropdown)
* Gender (Button slicer)

These filters allow users to dynamically explore the data.

---

### 🔹 Visualizations

* Attrition by Department
* Attrition by Job Role
* Attrition by Age Group
* Attrition by Income Group
* Attrition by OverTime
* Attrition by Job Satisfaction

---

## 🧠 Data Modeling & Transformations

* Created DAX measures:

  * Attrition Count
  * Attrition Rate
  * Active Employees
* Derived calculated columns:

  * Age Group
  * Income Group
  * Job Satisfaction Level (Low, Medium, High, Very High)
* Ensured proper sorting and formatting for better readability

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* Employees working **OverTime** show significantly higher attrition
* Lower **Job Satisfaction** is strongly associated with employees leaving
* Certain **Departments and Job Roles** experience higher turnover
* Employees in lower **Income Groups** are more likely to leave
* Younger employees (Under 30) show higher attrition trends
